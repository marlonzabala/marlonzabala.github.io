10 Steps To Becoming an Engaged, Inspired and Creative Lights On® Learner Through Passion Led Projects

INTRODUCTION

These 10 steps will enable you to focus on your children’s passions, steering them towards mastery learning for their natural born talent.

As a result, it will rekindle their desire to learn and make their education more purposeful.

Step 1

Empower your child to take full ownership of their learning adventure

For your children to really thrive as learners they have to connect with the bigger ‘why?’ and see the purpose behind their learning. Without this, they are missing an important piece of the puzzle - intrinsic motivation.

Step 2

Decide In Advance What They Want To Achieve

The more clarity your child has at the start of a project the more likely they are to push through to the end. So spend some time thinking about the end goal. Discuss what it is going to be like, how they want to feel at the start, mid and end of the project, and what they are going to do when things get difficult or challenging.

Step 3

Vision The End Goal And Break It Down Into Micro-Steps

Tuning into the power of visualization helps your child to keep seeing the bigger picture as their ultimate end goal. Then get them to think about ONE achievable step they need to take to move towards that destination. Keep going with one action step after another. In doing so you break the vision into micro-steps which don’t feel so daunting.

Step 4

Create A Positive Feedback Loop

Reflection is a huge part of our Lights On® framework as it reminds us to build celebration into the process of learning. Use kind, helpful specific critique as your child moves through the various stages of a project. This will help them make progress and also fire up their success circuit by rewarding their brain with dopamine rewards!

Step 5

Engage Fully In The Process

To keep the intrinsic motivation strong you want to constantly refer back to the WHY. The more real world a project is, and if you can build in an authentic audience even better, the more likely your child is to see the point of keeping going. Check in regularly to see if the action steps are taking them closer or further away from the desired outcome.

Step 6

Aim for Flow

The key to a successful Lights On® project is to find the sweet spot between boredom and challenge. You don’t want it to be too easy or boredom will set in, and if it is too challenging then it may make your child anxious that they won’t be able to achieve it. So play around by tweaking the conditions until you can see your child is back with their Lights On® and are getting in the flow!

Step 7

Imagine Your Success

About half way into a project the enthusiasm can wane - so help your child to keep the visualisation of the desired outcome in their mind and mentally rehearse BEING at that end destination. The more their brain believes it is a reality the more it will work for them and not against them. Encourage them to use all five senses when priming in their vision.

Step 8

Check In With Your Child’s Thoughts

Where are they telling themselves ‘it’s not good enough, I can’t do it, I don’t want to do it anymore, I’m bored of it now?’ Because their thoughts determine their reality by influencing what they believe is possible and the action they take, or don’t take, to get the results they want.

Step 9

Repeat and iterate

It is the deliberate practice over time that wires in success. This is where your child can use their brain's neuroplasticity to their advantage to build resilience. When they know they can’t expect to deliver their best work in one draft, it opens them up to exponential growth and mastery of learning.

Step 10

Support your child to Extend beyond their edges

Lights On® learning is all about going that little bit further than where you thought was possible. It is aiming to have at least one toe (if a whole foot feels too much) outside your comfort zone.
