CURIOSITY AUDIT

A step by step guide to finding out what your child and whole family are curious about whilst having an open and honest conversation together

INTRODUCTION

At Lights On we believe that every one of us has something that we were born to explore. So we take it as a given that every child has many things they want to learn about the world around them.

So we encourage parents to discover what their children are curious about.

Often a child, switched off from learning for years, will tell us that no-one has ever bothered to ask them.

When we have something we want to know the answer to we become highly curious. We also become internally driven to go deep into that adventure.

Step 1

Get a big sheet of paper and lots of colourful pens. Ask every member of your family to write or doodle down at least 10 things each they are curious about.

Step 2

Help each other out, if you get stuck. Remind them about all the questions they used to ask as a young child or in life before kids.

Step 3

Once you've got this all on to paper try to drill down a layer to specifics. So if your child says 'being outdoors' get curious by asking: 'what in particular about being outdoors?' They may say something like 'Building dens'.

If they have put baking, then ask 'what are you curious to bake?' Cakes? Any cakes or a specific kind of cake?' They may say something like 'Vegan cakes.'

There you have it...

Your family learning adventures roadmap that will help them get 'curiouser and curiouser' over time!

REFLECTIONS

Make some notes about how you felt you Curiosity Audit went, what you learnt about your family and yourself and what your next steps will be
