Be Curious
Our children are sharing their interests with us all the time, our role is to get curious,
listen and tune into any spark of an idea. Being curious and connecting with our
children can turn that spark into a new project to explore or simply provide your next
step forward.
It can be all too easy to miss the spark of an idea because we are busy looking for the
learning. The trick is to see what your children are curious about and encourage them
to pursue that idea with gusto. The learning will come as a result of them making their
idea a reality. Making mistakes, overcoming hurdles and adapting your plans are all part
of the journey. Get curious at every part of this journey and you will see new
opportunities for growth appear and a wealth of learning that is taking place.
Here are a few phrases/questions that might help you to get curious and ignite that
spark of an idea. You will notice that these phrases put the child in the driving seat,
remember it is important that they take ownership of their learning journey rather than
it being directed by others.
• I am all ears, tell me everything.
• Cool idea, I wonder how we could do that.
• That is a great idea, tell me all about it.
• That is so cool, we could start that now, what do you need?
• It sounds like you know what you want to do, can I help or are you okay on your
own?
• Wow I don’t know much about that, but I’d like to, can you share what you know
with me!
• You sound very excited about that; how can you make it happen?
• You should explore that idea, it sounds amazing. Where can you start?
• Did that idea just come to you, that idea needs someone like you to make it
happen. How cool would that be.
• That sounds very interesting, tell me more.
• Great idea, do you want to learn how to do it and then perhaps you can teach me?
• Sounds fun, you’re in charge so lead the way, what’s first?
• We are definitely going to need all our super brains for this idea, I’m ready, are
you?
