12 STRATEGIES TO GET YOUR CHILD
PASSIONATE ABOUT LEARNING
MY TOP 3 LIGHTS ON® TAKE AWAYS
1. Identify your own mindset and how this may be influencing you – if you have
a fixed mindset or are a perfectionist like me, it is likely you are going to find it
difficult if your well planned out idea backfires or things are not going as fast as
you would like. What worked well for me was spending time forming a good
understanding of my own mindset and how it influenced how I reacted to such
situations. I was then able to own my mindset and open up to Julia about it,
taking on board her suggestions. Also, by understanding and owning my mindset
I was able to start making small changes about myself. This naturally started to
have a positive impact on my daughter and the rest of the family.
2. Explore why you joined Light’s On® and what you want to achieve for your
child – I found it was easy to get caught up in looking at other people’s posts and
Julia’s feedback about their progress rather than focus on what I wanted to
achieve for my family. For a while I was comparing our lack of achievement with
their success and feeling a bit deflated about it. What helped in this situation was
remembering why we started this journey in the first place and what I wanted to
achieve from it. I stopped comparing and rushing to catch up and started to focus
on where in the journey we were and what we needed to do to get to the next
stage. Realising that it was not a race really helped to slow things down and allow
Amna to take the lead. It was then that I started to see the little bits of progress
she was making and share those little things rather than think it was not enough
of a thing to share.
3. Keep a journal of how certain things make YOU feel about your child’s
resistance – Rather than wonder why Amna was resisting or not wanting to go
back to something she had started I began to focus on how that made me feel. I
then went deeper by exploring why it made me feel that way. I found this so
helpful as it took the focus off Amna; I was able to stop seeing her as the problem
and focus on what I need to change.
I still believe Amna is full of potential and often feel frustrated when she doesn’t
want to engage or doesn’t give a task “her all”, but I am starting to see her learn in
so many other ways now; sometimes when we are engaged in Light’s On®
challenges or projects, but mainly in everyday life experiences. One of my
mantras is “the less you focus on the end result the quicker it will come”. Amna is
starting to enjoy learning again and is willing to take risks and make mistakes.
What’s more exciting, she is teaching the rest of the family how to do the same!
