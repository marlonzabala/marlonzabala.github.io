WE MADE HIM FEEL HEARD AND THAT HIS OPINIONS
MATTERED.
After years of trying to understand why my daughter was struggling with
anxiety, by reading books, talking to the teachers and seeking professional
help, I just wasn't getting anywhere. I then stumbled across Explorium and it
opened my eyes to the possibility that being part of the school system may
have actually played a large role in why she was struggling so much in the
first place.
Being part of Lights On® has turned out to be of immense value for our son
too. Some of his problems included; lacking self-esteem, not understanding
why he found the school environment difficult, being unmotivated to learn;
because a lot of the content he was being taught seemed so pointless. The
changes we've made have really helped transformed his attitude, confidence
and ability to see things in a more open way.
MY TOP 3 LIGHTS ON® TAKE AWAYS
1. Reframing what Archie understood about failure. We did a variety of
activities that emphasised the importance of failure and what it means, and
how it can actually benefit you and help you succeed.
2. Secondly, we set up a number of meetings with his school, so that he
could express his views about what was causing him so much anguish. We
made sure Archie was confident to be honest but also put forward solutions
which, I believe, made him feel heard and that his opinions mattered. Due to
the resistance we experienced, he also learnt that getting a no doesn't mean
you stop until a solution is found!
3. Stepping back and letting Archie take the lead. Rather than try to solve
the problems for him I have been able to empower him to solve them himself.
